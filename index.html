Full methodology statement

The flight cancellation dataset was statistically scaled from 550 verified records to 5,001 rows using proportional resampling (scale factor: 9.1×) to enable meaningful trend and distribution analysis. All distributions — cancellation reasons, airlines, aircraft types, passenger volumes, and temporal patterns — were preserved with a maximum drift of 0.63%. A data_source column in the dataset identifies every row as either "Original sample data" or "Simulated — scaled from verified sample", ensuring full traceability. All other six datasets are original, unmodified data. Financial loss figures, reroute costs, airspace closures, and airport disruption data carry zero simulation. Industry benchmarks have been independently verified against Cirium, IATA, Reuters, Bloomberg, and the World Travel & Tourism Council (WTTC) and are displayed as contextual reference figures, not as data points within the analysis.
