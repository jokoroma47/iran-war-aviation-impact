# Iran War — Global Aviation Impact

A data analytics project examining the operational and financial 
impact of the Iran conflict on global aviation between 
February and March 2026.

---

## Overview

When the Iran conflict escalated in late February 2026, the 
ripple effects on global aviation were immediate and severe. 
I built this project to quantify exactly what happened — 
which airlines were hit hardest, which airspace corridors 
collapsed, how passengers were affected, and what it all 
cost the industry.

The analysis draws on 7 datasets covering flight 
cancellations, reroutes, airspace closures, airport 
disruptions, airline financial losses, and the conflict 
timeline itself. All findings were cross-checked against 
published data from Cirium, IATA, Reuters and Bloomberg.

---

## What I Found

- Total estimated airline losses reached $8.15 billion 
  within 13 days
- The Middle East bore 90.6% of all financial losses
- Qatar Airways lost 41.5% of its revenue — the highest 
  of any carrier
- 23 countries enforced airspace closures, generating 
  39 individual closure events
- Rerouted flights added an average of 1,481 km and 
  $7,671 in extra fuel per flight
- Over 1.3 million passengers were affected, with the 
  1 million milestone reached by Day 11
- Emirates was burning $4.2 million per day at peak 
  disruption

## Data & Methodology

Six of the seven datasets used in this project are original, 
unmodified source data. The flight cancellations dataset was 
statistically scaled from 550 records to 5,001 rows to 
support meaningful trend analysis. The scaling preserved all 
distributions with a maximum drift of 0.63%, and every 
simulated row is clearly flagged in a data_source column. 
The methodology page inside the dashboard covers this in 
full detail.

Industry figures from Cirium, IATA, Reuters, Bloomberg and 
the WTTC are referenced as external benchmarks, not as 
data points within the analysis.
